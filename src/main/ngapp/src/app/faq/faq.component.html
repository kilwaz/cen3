FAQ

We kindly ask for all guests to arrive by 11.45am and be seated by 12noon latest. The Civil Ceremony must start on time (no indian timing!), so no latecomers will be allowed in the room after this time.
For the Mehndi night we have chosen for this to be a young adults evening only to allow this to be an informal party for friends and family to meet prior to the wedding day. Children are welcome to the Wedding and Reception but we kindly ask to please keep noise to a minimum during the Civil Ceremony. Activity packs will be provided in the evening to help keep them occupied!
You will have around 2 hours to relax (4pm-6pm) between the events of the day. You are welcome to stay in the venue where there will be lots of seating and refreshments available. Alternatively if you have a hotel booked nearby, feel free to go back and get ready for the evening events!
For the Mehndi night we have kept the dress code informal - you can wear Western or Traditional Indian dress as you prefer.
For the civil ceremony - this is a Western event so we ask as many guests as possible to wear Suit and Tie/Western dresses.
For the reception - this is an Indian event so we ask as many guests as possible to wear Tradition Indian dress or Formal attire.
Do not worry if you do not have the correct attire for the event - all guests are not expected to change between events!
For those who will be changing from Western attire to Indian attire for the evening, there will be changing rooms provided at the venue between 4pm-6pm to use if you do not have a hotel room nearby.
All food provided on the wedding day will be vegetarian. As our big day is a fusion of Western and Indian cultures, we will have an English afternoon tea after the Civil Ceremony and Traditional Indian food for the evening reception.
Please see our Accommodation page on the website for a list of suggested hotels nearby.
